Web Manual

This page gives a technical description of all elements generally available for creating Web content for Storebrand.

The design details are being worked out as you read this, so don't worry if something looks a bit odd.

Grid: Based on 12 columns from Bootstrap 3. We can responsively divide the contents horizontally into any combination of 12 in total (12, 6+6, 3+3+3+3, 4+4+4, and so on).

Our breakpoints are:
Extra small = up to 767px, Small = 768-991px, Medium = 992-1199px, Large = 1200px and up.

Alt om din pensjon

  • Hvordan får jeg oversikt over hva jeg får i pensjon? 1
    • Har du pensjon i Storebrand, logg inn på dine personlige sider for fullstendig oversikt. Der kan du også hente inn tallene dine fra folketrygden (NAV).
    • Har du fripoliser eller pensjonskapitalbevis fra tidligere arbeidsgivere, finner du disse på minpensjon.no
    • Prøv vår kalkulator som enkelt viser deg hva du kan forvente å få i pensjon. Vi gir deg også en vurdering av hvor mye du bør spare for å få den pensjonen du ønsker deg.
  • Hva består egentlig pensjon av? 2
    Pensjon består av:
    1. Folketrygden - opptjening betalt av staten. Hvor mye du får, kommer an på hvor gammel du er, ditt lønnsnivå og hvor lenge du har jobbet.
    2. Tjenestepensjon - dette betales av din arbeidsgiver. Her finnes det forskjellige avtaler og det lønner seg å være bevisst på hva du får fra din arbeidsgiver.
    3. AFP - Avtalefestet pensjon. Betales av arbeidsgiver, men fungerer litt som folketrygden. Ikke alle har denne avtalen, så undersøk hos din arbeidsgiver.

(Done-ish)

Increasingly, the best choice is to use native form elements. For advanced form elements, this is doubly so.

Popover

(Done-ish)

Note: Popover does not have namespaced class names; markup/class names are inside Bootstrap javascript-files. We simply override the CSS with the same classnames as default BS.

Display of popover can be done on click, hover or focus for the element. For full documentation, see Bootstrap.

If there's not enough room to show the popover at the desired placement (top, bottom, right, left), the popover will automatically fail to an alternative placement.

Examples (please hover for demo):            Link with hover-popover

Popover requires Bootstrap's popover.js + tooltip.js. Also, note that standard Bootstrap offers tooltip, which is just a simpler version of popover. We have deliberately not included tooltip in our web manual, because popover probably covers our needs.

        id="demo-popover"
        data-toggle="popover"
        data-trigger="hover"
        data-content="Mr. Fogg learned all this in consulting his Bradshaw."
        data-placement="auto top">Hover for popover
</button>
<script>
  // use this in document.ready or similar:
  $('#demo-popover').popover();

Title Tag

(Up for debate)

BZT: This does not belong here, and will be moved to usability wiki. This design manual is for describing technical markup.

Title tags—technically called title elements—define the title of a document. Title tags are often used on search engine results pages (SERPs) to display preview snippets for a given page, and are important both for SEO and social sharing.

The title element of a web page is meant to be an accurate and concise description of a page's content. This element is critical to both user experience and search engine optimization. It creates value in three specific areas: relevancy, browsing, and in the search engine results pages.

Optimal Format

Primary Keyword - Secondary Keyword | Brand Name

Code sample:

                      <head>
                          <title>Pensjon - Ekstrapensjon | storebrand.no</title>
                      </head>

Optimal Length for Search Engines

Google typically displays the first 50-60 characters of a title tag, or as many characters as will fit into a 512-pixel display. If you keep your titles under 55 characters, you can expect at least 95% of your titles to display properly. Keep in mind that search engines may choose to display a different title than what you provide in your HTML. Titles in search results may be rewritten to match your brand, the user query, or other considerations.

A title tag is the main text that describes an online document. Title elements have long been considered one of the most important on-page SEO elements (the most important being overall content), and appear in three key places: browsers, search engine results pages, and external websites.

Title tags often show up in both the top of a browser's chrome and in tabs.

Links

Many external websites—especially social media sites—will use the title tag of a web page as its link anchor text.

Example:
The page title is "Pensjon - Egen sparing til pensjon | storebrand.no" and the <h1> on the page is "Egen sparing til pensjon" links to this page should be the same

                      <head>
                          <title>Pensjon - Egen sparing til pensjon | storebrand.no</title>
                      </head>

                      <h1>Egen sparing til pensjon</h1>

                      <a href="pensjon_privatpensjonssparing.html">Egen sparing til pensjon</a>
